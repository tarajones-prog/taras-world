# ESO Fee Structure

**Program:** Easy Street Offers (ESO) Pilot
**Fee type:** Assignment fee paid to ESO partner (DNMN Investments LLC) at closing

---

## Overview

When Opendoor acquires a property through the ESO channel, an assignment fee is paid to ESO at the time of closing. This fee compensates ESO for sourcing the deal, negotiating the purchase contract with the seller, managing EMD and title, and executing the assignment to Opendoor.

---

## Fee Structure

| Component | Description |
|-----------|-------------|
| **Fee type** | Assignment fee — percentage of the final purchase price |
| **Fee range** | 1–3% (varies per deal, defined in Addendum A of the ESO contract) |
| **Paid to** | DNMN Investments LLC (ESO's operating entity) |
| **When paid** | At Close of Escrow, on the Final Settlement Statement (FSS) |
| **Line item on FSS** | Fee to DNMN — see current accounting guidance for correct FSS placement |

---

---

## ABAD (Assignment Between Assignor and Buyer)

The ABAD is a separate document from the purchase contract. It formalizes the assignment of the contract from ESO (DNMN Investments LLC) to Opendoor.

- Sent to seller by ESO at the time of assignment
- Must be fully executed before closing
- Contains the assignment fee amount and payment terms

---

## Key Document Reference

- **ESO Contract Terms "Addendum A"** — Defines fee terms for each deal
- **MASTER NATIONAL ASSIGNMENT KIT** — Contains standard ABAD language
- **FSS** — Final Settlement Statement where fee is recorded
