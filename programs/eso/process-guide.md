# ESO Process Guide — 12-Step Intake and Acquisition Flow

**Trigger:** Receive Open Escrow email from ESO partner (transactions@easystreetoffers.com)

---

## Pre-Flight Checklist

Before starting the CITY intake, confirm:
- [ ] Property is on the ESO weekly list that Pricing reviewed
- [ ] Offer is in "Accepted" state in CITY (has a Final Offer)
- [ ] ESO contract is executed and ready to upload

---

## Step-by-Step Flow

### Step 1 — Receive Open Escrow Notification
ESO sends an open escrow email to the Opendoor ESO inbox when they've opened title on a property. This is the trigger to begin CITY intake.

### Step 2 — Verify CITY Offer State
Navigate to the CITY Acquisition offer page for the property.
- Offer must be in **"Accepted"** state (has Final Offer)
- If not yet Accepted, do not proceed — coordinate with Pricing

### Step 3 — Apply the ESO Label
In CITY, set the label to: **"Agent Acq - Outbound"**
This ensures the deal is correctly categorized for tracking and reporting.

### Step 4 — Start Contract in CITY
- Customer Dashboard → Start Contract → Choose **Cash** product
- Do not choose Cash Plus unless explicitly directed

### Step 5 — Upload Executed ESO Contract
- Go to the **Documents** tab in CITY
- Upload the executed ESO purchase contract
- This is the contract between ESO (DNMN Investments LLC) and the seller

### Step 6 — Assign Roles
In CITY → Manage Roles, assign:
- **Acquisition CX** → Acquisition Home Advisor (Jackson)
- **Acquisition Home Advisor** → Jackson
- **Acquisition Closing Manager** → Jackson

### Step 7 — Update Seller Contact
- Navigate to Seller Participants
- Set primary seller contact to the **ESO email group** (internal ops email)
- This ensures ESO receives all seller-side communications until assignment is complete

### Step 8 — Disable Digital Checkout
- Disable the Digital Checkout requirement in CITY
- ESO transactions are not consumer-facing — digital checkout doesn't apply

### Step 9 — Schedule Diligence Visit
- Go to the **Assessments** tab → Add → Diligence Visit
- Schedule via **Inspectify** (contact: Becky Trayler)
- DV task will auto-populate in the CITY workflow
- Communicate the scheduled inspection date to ESO (transactions@easystreetoffers.com)

### Step 10 — Diligence and Negotiation
- DV result comes in from Inspectify
- ESO negotiates any diligence repair requests directly with the seller (BINSR process)
- Executed diligence paperwork is sent to the ESO partner email

### Step 11 — Assignment Agreement (≤7 Days Before COE)
**Critical timing:** Assignment must be executed **no later than 7 days before Close of Escrow.**

Steps:
1. Pull the appropriate assignment agreement from the MASTER NATIONAL ASSIGNMENT KIT
2. Send to ESO for execution
3. ESO sends ABAD (Assignment Between Assignor and Buyer) to the seller at time of assignment
4. Buyer docs go to Opendoor; earnest money transfers; assignment fee goes on Final Settlement Statement (FSS) payable to DNMN Investments LLC

### Step 12 — Post-Assignment Update
After assignment is complete:
- Update seller contact in CITY from the ESO email to the **listing agent** (if applicable)
- Add entry to the "Easy Street Acquisitions" tracking sheet
- Note any process gaps in "Easy Street Deals" process doc for future improvement

## Timing Reference

| Milestone | Timing Requirement |
|-----------|-------------------|
| Open Escrow notification → CITY intake | As soon as possible |
| DV scheduling | Immediately after Step 8 |
| Assignment Agreement execution | ≤7 business days before COE |
| EMD confirmation at title | At contract open |

---

## Escalation

If any step is blocked or delayed, see [escalation-playbook.md](escalation-playbook.md).

---

## Key Lesson

> EMD timing and assignment execution are the two most time-sensitive steps — delays here cascade. Build reminders for: assignment ≤7 days before COE, EMD confirmed at title.
