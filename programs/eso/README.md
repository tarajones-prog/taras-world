# Easy Street Offers (ESO) — Program Overview

**Status:** Active Pilot
**Type:** Acquisition channel — assignable contracts
**Last updated:** 2026-03

---

## What Is ESO?

Easy Street Offers (ESO) is an external partner that sources off-market properties from sellers, enters into assignable purchase contracts, and then assigns those contracts to Opendoor as the ultimate buyer — no later than 7 days before Close of Escrow (COE).

This is an **acquisition channel**, not a listing program. ESO does the seller-facing work; Opendoor is the ultimate buyer.

**ESO entity name:** DNMN Investments LLC
**Assignment fee:** Paid to ESO at closing (see [fee-structure.md](fee-structure.md))

---

## Why This Matters

ESO expands Opendoor's acquisition pipeline by sourcing deals that come in through ESO's agent and seller network — properties that might not have come to Opendoor directly. It's a force-multiplier for acquisitions.

---

## How It Works (High Level)

1. ESO identifies potential properties and presents them to Opendoor Pricing weekly
2. Pricing selects targets; ESO negotiates the contract with the seller
3. ESO opens title and sends earnest money deposit (EMD)
4. Opendoor receives notice and begins the intake process in CITY
5. Inspection (Diligence Visit) is scheduled via Inspectify
6. ESO handles diligence negotiations with seller
7. Assignment Agreement is sent ≤7 days before COE
8. Closing proceeds; ESO receives assignment fee at settlement

Full step-by-step: [process-guide.md](process-guide.md)
