# HSA Agent Acquisition Training Guide

**Audience:** Home Sales Associates (HSAs)
**Purpose:** Equip HSAs to proactively engage agents and explain Opendoor's acquisition programs effectively.

---

## Core Training Areas

### 1. CNML (Cash Now More Later) Positioning

**What is CNML?**
CNML (Cash Now More Later) — previously called Cash+ — is an Opendoor product where sellers can:
- Accept a cash offer from Opendoor now (certainty of sale)
- Participate in upside if Opendoor sells the property for more than the purchase price

**Why agents care:**
- Gives their seller clients a third option beyond "list it" or "take a cash offer"
- Reduces seller anxiety about leaving money on the table
- Still closes quickly (cash offer certainty)

**Positioning for agents:**
- Lead with the client benefit: certainty + potential upside
- Acknowledge the tradeoff: client gives up control of the listing
- Emphasize speed and simplicity compared to traditional listing

**Common objections and responses:**
| Objection | Response Framework |
|-----------|-------------------|
| "My client can get more by listing" | Acknowledge — CNML still offers upside potential, plus closing certainty |
| "What happens if Opendoor doesn't sell for more?" | Client still gets the agreed cash price — no downside |
| "Is this available in my market?" | Check current market availability with your market's list |

### 2. Agent Incentive Structure (1% Program)

See [commission-structure.md](commission-structure.md) for full details.

**Quick pitch for agents:**
- Agents earn a commission when they bring a seller who closes with Opendoor
- The incentive is designed to reward proactive agent engagement
- Payment is made after closing — no risk to the agent

**What HSAs need to know:**
- Confirm the agent is registered or walk them through registration
- Explain the payment process so agents understand how they get paid
- Set expectations on timing (payment after closing, not at contract)

---

### 3. Proactive Call Volume

**Why this matters:**
Agent acquisition is a numbers game. Agents who have heard of Opendoor's programs are far more likely to recommend them to clients. Proactive calls build that awareness.

**Call goals:**
- Not every call needs to result in a referral
- Goal is to build awareness, answer questions, and be top-of-mind when an agent has a CNML-fit seller
- Follow up after key market events (price reductions, new inventory)

**What to cover on a proactive agent call:**
1. Introduce yourself and your role at Opendoor
2. Ask about their current seller clients and deal flow
3. Introduce CNML if it's a fit for any of their clients
4. Explain the agent incentive briefly
5. Offer to answer any questions about the process
6. Set a follow-up touchpoint

## Training Scenarios

### Scenario 1 — Agent Is Skeptical
> Agent says: "I've had sellers use Opendoor before and they weren't happy with the price."

Response framework:
- Acknowledge the concern genuinely
- Explain that CNML addresses this with the upside component
- Offer to walk through a specific example if they have a current seller in mind

### Scenario 2 — Agent Is Curious But Doesn't Understand CNML
> Agent says: "What's the difference between Opendoor's regular cash offer and this new thing?"

Response framework:
- CNML = cash offer certainty + upside participation
- Standard cash offer = certainty only, no upside
- Both are fast and simple compared to traditional listing

### Scenario 3 — Agent Wants to Know About Their Commission
> Agent says: "So how do I actually get paid?"

Response framework:
- Payment comes after successful closing
- Payment goes to the broker entity
- HSA can connect them with the payments team for details
