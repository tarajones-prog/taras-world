# Referral Payments — Workflow and Tracker

**Program:** Agent Referral Program
**Payment:** Referral fee to agent/broker when a referred seller closes with Opendoor
**Vehicle:** Check (legacy) or Ramp PO

---

## Overview

When an agent refers a seller to Opendoor and that seller successfully closes, the agent/broker receives a referral fee. The Program Manager tracks, processes, and pays these fees.

---

## Step-by-Step Referral Payment Flow

### Step 1 — Verify the Referral
Confirm the referral is eligible:
- Agent was registered before the referral was submitted
- Seller closed with Opendoor (confirmed close date)
- No prior payment for this closing exists

Sources:
- "Monthly Partner Payments Tracker" (Google Drive) — payment status
- "Check Request Form v.2 (Responses)" (Google Drive) — original payment requests

### Step 2 — Confirm Agent / Broker Information
- Legal entity name
- Payment address (for check) or preferred payment method
- Prior payment history

> **Hard rule: Always confirm address before processing any payment.**

### Step 3 — Process Payment
**Via Ramp PO (preferred):**
1. Create PO in Ramp
2. Payee = broker legal entity name
3. Memo = referral program name + property address
4. Submit and confirm

**Via check (legacy):**
- Coordinate with Finance for check issuance
- Confirm delivery address
- Track check number in payment tracker

### Step 4 — Update Tracker
In "Monthly Partner Payments Tracker":
- Mark as paid
- Record payment method, date, and reference number
- Note any issues

---

## Incoming Payment Without Address

**Situation:** Opendoor receives a referral payment from a broker but there's no property address in the memo.

**Steps:**
1. Check the "Monthly Partner Payments Tracker" for any expected payments from this entity
2. Check "Check Request Form v.2" for pending requests
3. Reach out to the broker/agent: "We received a payment from your entity — can you confirm which property this relates to?"
4. Hold the payment in reconciliation until address is confirmed
5. Document in tracker with note: "Address pending from [broker name]"

> **Known open item:** A Realty of America payment received in October 2025 still has no address attribution. This is the canonical example of why address-in-memo is a hard rule.
